Welcome

Geographic Information System (GIS) is a powerful technology used to collect, store, analyze and display geographical data. GIS plays an important role in environmental conservation and sustainable development.

About GIS

GIS combines maps, satellite images, databases and analytical tools to understand spatial information. It helps researchers, planners and environmental managers make better decisions.

Applications of GIS in Environmental Conservation

  • Monitoring deforestation and forest resources.
  • Mapping flood-prone areas.
  • Wildlife habitat management.
  • Water resource management.
  • Land use planning.
  • Climate change analysis.
  • Environmental impact assessment.

Benefits of GIS

  • Improves decision making.
  • Provides accurate spatial information.
  • Saves time and resources.
  • Supports sustainable environmental management.
